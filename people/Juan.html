Juan Francisco Camacho

Adjudicator · Pianist & Educator

Juan Camacho is a Colombian pianist who has performed extensively as a soloist and collaborative artist in major cities across Colombia and the United States. Recent U.S. appearances include performances at the Abraham Lincoln Presidential Library, the Governor’s Mansion in Springfield, the UIS Visual Arts Gallery, the UIS Performing Arts Center, and Smith Memorial Hall at the University of Illinois. Since 2021, he has served as Piano Instructor at the University of Illinois Springfield, where he teaches individual and group piano. He has also collaborated with Concerto Urbano at the University of Illinois Urbana–Champaign and was featured as accompanist in Music of Hope at the Hoogland Center for the Arts and UIS (2023).

Camacho was selected to perform Mozart’s Piano Concerto No. 13 in C Major with the Camerata Olav Roots at the Fabio Lozano Auditorium of the Jorge Tadeo Lozano University and has appeared as soloist at the prestigious León de Greiff Hall. His versatility extends to historical keyboards—performing Bach’s Concerto BWV 1057 on harpsichord and Handel’s Concerto Op. 4 No. 5 on organ at the National University of Colombia. He recently played continuo in the Baroque Chamber Music Bootcamp at Harvard’s Memorial Church (2025). He has also focused on the works of Colombian composer Mauricio Nasi, premiering his Toccata No. 30 and Toccata No. 27 at the National University of Colombia, and is currently performing his Fantasía Romántica Op. 151.

Camacho has developed an introductory harmony curriculum that helps keyboard students internalize harmonic practices and voice-leading patterns. He is pursuing a Doctor of Musical Arts in Piano Performance and Literature as a Ross Fellow at the University of Illinois under Dr. Timothy Ehlen, and holds an MM from Missouri State University with Dr. Hye-Jung Hong and a bachelor’s degree from the National University of Colombia with Prof. Juan Pablo Luna.
